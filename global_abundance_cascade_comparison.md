## Detailed Civilization Analysis

### 1. ZHOU DYNASTY (China, 1046-256 BCE)
**Duration: 790 years**

#### Build Phase (1046 BCE):
- **Military conquest** of Shang Dynasty
- **Feudal system** with kinship bonds (fengjian policy)
- **S-axis:** Land grants to nobles
- **R-axis:** "Deference to the king and his Mandate + reverence for shared history—including deceased spirits of related ancestors"
- **Religious legitimacy:** Mandate of Heaven

#### Abundance Phase (1046-771 BCE): **275 years**
**The Evidence:**
> "The fengjian policy was so successful, producing such abundance of crops, that the resultant prosperity validated the Zhou as possessing the Mandate of Heaven"

> "Agricultural technology advanced a lot under the Eastern Zhou dynasty. Better farming output means more people can live on the same farm, so population grew greatly"

**Result:** 
- Peak prosperity and validation
- Population growth
- Cultural flourishing

#### Decouple Phase (771-475 BCE): **296 years**
**The Mechanism:**
> "First, ties of kinship so crucial to the founding of Zhou lost their meaning over time. In later generations, lords simply became more interested in and identified with their own territories, and they had little sense of solidarity with what were at best distant cousins ruling neighboring feudal states"

> "The traditional, hierarchical Zhou society couldn't handle this [abundance]. The nobles, who were from the Zhou tribe, were forced to open their ranks to the commoners, who were conquered subjects. They weren't that respectful of their masters, so the whole empire started weakening"

**R-Axis Failure:**
- 771 BCE: King You killed, capital sacked
- Lords stopped paying visits to king's court
- Kinship bonds became meaningless
- Religious/ancestral R-axis collapsed

#### Collapse (Warring States Period, 475-256 BCE): **219 years**
**The Brutal Statistics:**
- **540 wars fought over 200 years** before Warring States period
- **ONE MAJOR BATTLE PER YEAR** for 250 years during Warring States
- 150 feudal states → 15 states → 1 state (Qin)
- Final Zhou king killed 256 BCE

**Total Timeline:** 
- Abundance to final collapse: **775 years**
- R-axis failure to collapse: **515 years**

### 2. ABBASID CALIPHATE (Islamic, 750-1258 CE)
**Duration: 508 years**

#### Build Phase (750-762 CE):
- **Revolution** against Umayyad Caliphate
- **Baghdad established** as "City of Peace"
- **S-axis:** Persian bureaucracy, centralized administration
- **R-axis:** Islamic unity, claimed descent from Prophet's uncle
- **Religious legitimacy:** Family connection to Prophet Muhammad

#### Abundance/Golden Age (762-833 CE): **71 years**
**The Evidence:**
> "Agricultural innovations led to increased agricultural productivity, ensuring food security and supporting population growth"

> "Economic prosperity was bolstered by innovations in banking, finance, and agriculture"

> "Trade flourished across the Silk Roads and Indian Ocean, facilitating cultural exchange and economic prosperity"

> "The Abbasids raised the prestige and power of the empire, promoting commerce, industry, arts, and science, particularly during the reigns of al-Manṣūr, Hārūn al-Rashīd, and al-Maʾmūn"

**House of Wisdom:** Peak of Islamic scholarship, translation of Greek/Persian texts

#### Decouple Phase (833-945 CE): **112 years**
**The Mechanism:**
> "Their temporal power began to decline when al-Muʿtaṣim introduced non-Muslim Berber, Slav, and especially Turkish mercenary forces into his personal army"

> "Although these troops were converted to Islam, **the base of imperial unity through religion was gone**, and some of the new army officers quickly learned to control the caliphate through assassination of any caliph who would not accede to their demands"

**CRITICAL R-AXIS FAILURE (833 CE):**
- Islamic unity destroyed by mercenary forces
- Even though converted to Islam, they had NO religious bond to the Caliphate
- Army officers controlled caliphs through assassination
- Religious legitimacy became hollow

#### Prophet/Warning Phase (945-1055 CE):
- Būyids enter Baghdad 945 CE
- Caliphs become figureheads
- Seljuk Turks attempt to restore order (1055)
- Brief revival under al-Nasir (1180-1225)

#### Final Collapse (1258 CE):
**Mongol Invasion:**
- Baghdad sacked February 13, 1258
- Hundreds of thousands killed
- Caliph al-Musta'sim trampled to death in carpet
- House of Wisdom destroyed
- End of Islamic Golden Age

**Total Timeline:**
- Abundance to final collapse: **496 years**
- R-axis failure to collapse: **425 years**

### 3. KANEM-BORNU EMPIRE (African, 700-1387 CE)
**Duration: 687 years (First Kanem phase)**

#### Build Phase (700-1085 CE):
- **Nomadic Kanembu** establish capital at Njimi
- **Divine kings** (mais) of Duguwa dynasty
- **S-axis:** Trans-Saharan trade control, salt industry
- **R-axis:** Traditional Mune cult + divine kingship
- **Religious legitimacy:** Kings regarded as divine, "power over life and death"

#### Abundance Phase (1085-1259 CE): **174 years**
**The Evidence:**
> "The prosperity that Alawma brought to Kanem-Bornu continued throughout the 17th century"

> "The salt industry was particularly prosperous, producing perhaps 6500–9000 tons per year. The empire likely supplied salt across the entire surrounding region"

> "Under Mai Umme (late 11th century), Islam was adopted and Kanem-Bornu was an Islamic state, serving as a point of contact in trade between North Africa, the Nile River valley, and the sub-Sahara region"

**Peak Under Mai Dunama Dabbalemi (1221-1259):**
- Diplomatic exchanges with North Africa
- Special hostel established in Cairo for Mecca pilgrims
- Extended period of conquest (jihad declared)
- **CRITICAL ERROR:** Destroyed the Mune cult

#### Decouple Phase (1259-1380 CE): **121 years**
**The Mechanism:**
> "However, he also **destroyed the national Mune cult and thus precipitated widespread revolt** culminating in the uprise of the Tubu and the Bulala"

> "He assigned the conquered regions to the general who conquered them creating a nepotistic tendency which persevered even after his death"

**R-AXIS FAILURE (~1259 CE):**
- Traditional religious system (Mune cult) destroyed at peak of power
- Forced full Islamization alienated traditional power base
- Widespread revolt immediately followed
- Nepotism replaced kinship-based system

**Collapse Sequence:**
- Between 1359-1383: **Seven mais reigned, five killed by Bulala invaders**
- 1380: Forced to abandon Njimi, flee to Bornu
- Lost original homeland

#### Bornu Revival (1460-1600s):
- Managed to reconquer Kanem
- Mai Idris Alawma (1571-1603) brought second peak
- But empire never recovered original strength

#### Final Decline (1600s-1900):
- 17th century: Famine, trade route changes
- 18th century: Further weakening
- 1808: Defeated by Fulani jihad
- 1846: Sayfawa dynasty ends
- 1900: French conquest

**Total Timeline:**
- Abundance to first collapse: **295 years**
- R-axis failure to first collapse: **128 years**
