## **PART I: THE BIBLICAL BLUEPRINT**

### **A. Ezekiel's Temple Vision: The Primary Source**

**The Foundation Pattern:** Ezekiel 40-47 provides the basic narrative structure that Smith adapted for Lehi's dream.

#### **Divine Guide Leading Through Sacred Space**
- **EZEKIEL 40:3-4:** *"And he brought me thither, and, behold, there was a man, whose appearance was like the appearance of brass, with a line of flax and a measuring reed; and he stood in the gate. And the man said unto me, Son of man, behold with thine eyes, and hear with thine ears"*
- **1 NEPHI 8:5-6:** *"And it came to pass that I saw a man, and he was dressed in a white robe; and he came and stood before me. And it came to pass that he spake unto me, and bade me follow him."*

**Analysis:** Both visions feature an otherworldly guide who leads the prophet through sacred geography, instructing them to observe carefully.

#### **River Flowing from Sacred Source**
- **EZEKIEL 47:1:** *"Afterward he brought me again unto the door of the house; and, behold, waters issued out from under the threshold of the house eastward"*

And I looked to behold from whence it came; and I saw the head thereof a little way off"*

#### **Trees with Healing Fruit**
- **EZEKIEL 47:12:** *"And by the river upon the bank thereof, on this side and on that side, shall grow all trees for meat, whose leaf shall not fade, neither shall the fruit thereof be consumed: it shall bring forth new fruit according to his months, because their waters they issued out of the sanctuary: and the fruit thereof shall be for meat, and the leaf thereof for medicine"*

### **B. Adam Clarke's Commentary: The Smoking Gun**

**MOST DAMNING EVIDENCE:** Clarke's commentary explicitly connects Ezekiel 47 with Revelation 22, creating the exact synthesis Smith used:

**CLARKE ON EZEKIEL 47:** *"With respect to the phraseology of this chapter, it may be said that St. John had it particularly in view while he wrote his celebrated description of the paradise of God, Revelation 22. The prophet may therefore be referring to the same thing which the apostle describes, viz., the grace of the Gospel, and its effects in the world."*

**This is precisely the theological framework Smith adopted:** connecting Old Testament temple imagery with New Testament tree of life symbolism through Gospel metaphors.
